Operational Resilience and Data Management
This section covers the procedural and strategic measures used to handle data safely, manage risks, and ensure an organization can survive a cyber incident.
Information Security (InfoSec)
is a broad category that protects the integrity and privacy of data, both in storage and in transit. While network security protects the "pipes," InfoSec protects the actual data flowing through them. This involves encryption, identity management, and classification policies to ensure that sensitive information—such as user records or intellectual property—is not leaked or stolen.
Operational Security (OpSec)
involves the processes and decisions for handling and protecting data assets. This is often the human element of security; it includes analyzing risk, granting permissions to users, and determining how and where data may be stored or shared. It ensures that people do not accidentally expose sensitive information through negligence or poor procedure.
Disaster Recovery
defines how an organization responds to a cyber-security incident or any other event that causes the loss of operations or data. Disaster recovery policies dictate how the organization restores its data and operations to the same capacity as before the event. This is crucial for business continuity, ensuring that a ransomware attack or server failure does not result in permanent closure.
