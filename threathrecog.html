Threat Recognition and Avoidance
This section highlights the importance of identifying external dangers, such as malicious actors and deceptive tactics used to steal information or cause harm.
Recognize Scams & Phishing
is a critical skill for digital literacy. Phishing attacks often arrive via email or text message, masquerading as legitimate companies (like banks or delivery services) to trick users into clicking malicious links or revealing login credentials. A safe user knows how to spot the red flags: urgent language, strange URLs, poor grammar, and requests for sensitive financial information.
Be Wary of Strangers
is a rule that applies as strictly online as it does in the physical world. The anonymity of the internet allows malicious individuals to use "catfishing" techniques, pretending to be someone they are not to gain trust. Users should be skeptical of unsolicited friend requests or messages from unknown individuals, especially those who try to move the conversation to a private channel or ask for money or photos quickly.
Social Engineering Awareness
involves understanding that hackers often hack people rather than computers. By manipulating human emotions—fear, curiosity, or helpfulness—scammers can convince users to bypass their own security measures. Avoiding these threats requires a healthy dose of skepticism and a pause-and-think approach before reacting to any unexpected digital interaction.
