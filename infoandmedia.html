Information & Media Literacy
The section explores how individuals evaluate, understand, and responsibly use digital information and media in an increasingly complex online environment.
Information literacy
involves the ability to locate, evaluate, and effectively use information across digital platforms. In today's interconnected world, understanding how search engines work, how data is organized, and how to assess the reliability of online content is essential. Learners must discern credible sources from misleading ones, enabling them to make informed decisions in academic, professional, and everyday contexts.
Analyze and verify digital information
Equally important is the capacity to analyze and verify digital information. This includes understanding bias, recognizing persuasive techniques, and comparing multiple sources to validate facts. With the rise of misinformation and the ease of sharing content online, developing critical thinking skills becomes a core part of digital learning.
Media literacy
expands this skill set by enabling individuals to interpret and produce digital media responsibly. It involves understanding how media messages are constructed, how they influence audiences, and how to engage with them ethically. Media-literate individuals can create meaningful content—videos, graphics, posts—while also being aware of digital footprints and privacy implications.
