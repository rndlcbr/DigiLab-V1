Understanding Cybersecurity and Cybersafety

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. Its primary goal is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information (the CIA Triad) against threat actors who seek to access, change, or destroy sensitive data. These attacks can range from simple phishing attempts aimed at stealing personal passwords to sophisticated ransomware attacks designed to paralyze entire organizations or critical infrastructure. Cybersafety extends this concept to the human element, focusing on the responsible and safe use of technology to protect one's personal identity and mental well-being online.

The Multi-Layered Defense Strategy

Implementing effective cybersecurity requires a multi-layered approach where people, processes, and technology work together. It is not just about installing antivirus software; it involves securing the entire environment—from the network infrastructure and cloud storage to the individual applications users interact with daily. As illustrated in the previous image, this defense system must be resilient enough to detect threats in real-time and robust enough to recover quickly if a breach occurs. In an era where our lives are increasingly digital, maintaining strong cybersecurity hygiene is essential to prevent financial loss, reputational damage, and the disruption of daily life.
