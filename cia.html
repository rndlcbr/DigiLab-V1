The CIA Triad Framework
This section outlines the foundational model of information security policies used to guide the security efforts of an organization.
Confidentiality
is roughly equivalent to privacy. Measures undertaken to ensure confidentiality are designed to prevent sensitive information from reaching the wrong people while making sure that the right people can, in fact, get it. Access must be restricted to those authorized to view the data in question, often achieved through passwords, biometrics, and two-factor authentication.
Integrity
involves maintaining the consistency, accuracy, and trustworthiness of data over its entire lifecycle. Data must not be changed in transit, and steps must be taken to ensure that data cannot be altered by unauthorized people (for example, in a breach of financial records). File permissions and user access controls are employed to prevent unauthorized data modification.
Availability
ensures that the information is accessible when authorized users need it. This means that the hardware, technical infrastructure, and systems that hold and display the information must be maintained and functioning correctly. This pillar focuses on preventing "Denial of Service" (DDoS) attacks and ensuring systems have redundancy so that hardware failure does not result in downtime.
