Digital Citizenship and Etiquette
This section covers the behavioral aspects of internet use, focusing on the long-term impact of online actions and the responsibility to treat others with respect.
Think Before You Post
is the golden rule of managing one’s "digital footprint." Everything posted online—photos, comments, and status updates—can potentially exist forever and be seen by unintended audiences, including future employers or school admissions officers. Safer internet use involves considering the long-term consequences of content before hitting "send," ensuring that a momentary lapse in judgment does not permanently damage one's reputation.
Digital Citizenship
refers to the responsible use of technology to engage in society. It implies acting with integrity, empathy, and respect toward others in digital spaces. A good digital citizen stands up against cyberbullying, reports harmful content, and refuses to participate in the spread of toxicity or harassment. It is about making the internet a safer, more inclusive place for everyone, not just protecting oneself.
Information Validity
is also a part of responsible behavior. In an age of "fake news" and viral rumors, a responsible user verifies information before sharing it. Spreading misinformation can cause panic or harm to others. Thinking before posting also applies to the accuracy of the content, ensuring that you are contributing positively to the information ecosystem rather than adding to the noise.
